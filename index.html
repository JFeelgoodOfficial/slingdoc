How it works

Three simple steps. No new app.

  1. 01

    Worker texts & snaps

    Workers text IN or OUT and snap a photo of required documents — a W-4, certification, voided check. No app. No login. No training.

    SMS · RCS · Photo
  2. 02

    SlingDoc processes

    SlingDoc confirms via SMS, tracks the time entry, catalogs the document, and surfaces anything outstanding — all in real time on the manager dashboard.

    Real-time confirmation
  3. 03

    Systems sync

    Payroll, HR, and accounting receive clean data in the shape they already expect. No rip-and-replace. No new terminology. Integration lives in SlingDoc, not in your team.

Frequently asked

Common questions about SlingDoc.

Does SlingDoc require workers to download an app?
No. SlingDoc works entirely over SMS and MMS. Workers use the messaging app that already comes on their phone — nothing to install, no account to create, no password to remember.
Is SlingDoc compatible with screen readers?
Yes. SlingDoc is designed for blind and visually impaired workers from the start. Because it runs inside the phone's native Messages app, it inherits whatever screen reader, voice assistant, or accessibility setting the user already has configured — VoiceOver, TalkBack, or otherwise — instead of requiring a separate accessible interface.
What if a worker is on a job site with no cell data?
SMS works on almost any cellular signal — including signals too weak to load data-based apps. Workers can clock in by text even on low-connectivity sites. Photo attachments send when signal is available; the clock-in timestamp is recorded at the moment of texting.
What payroll and HR systems does SlingDoc integrate with?
SlingDoc syncs with major payroll, HR, and accounting platforms. Time records, document metadata, and worker profiles flow into the systems your finance and HR teams already use, so you do not need to rip out existing tooling.
Who built SlingDoc?
SlingDoc was built by Alejandro Argüello and the team at Core Goals Consulting, based in San Antonio and Austin, Texas. Alejandro previously helped build the banking industry's first 100% no-touch loan-origination system and holds multiple patents in transactional document processing.
Where is worker data stored?
Worker data and document captures are stored on AWS infrastructure with 99.99% durability and accessed through a secure cloud dashboard for managers.
